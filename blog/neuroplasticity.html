1. Research Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people:

The principles of Neuroplasticity help people in the sense that it allows individuals to make new pathways in the brain which may help individuals to learn new skills quicker than if the brain was at fixed state and couldn't make new pathways/connections.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit:

By trying out new activities that the brain isn't used to performing; the brain will make new pathways ,or strengthen pathways that already exist, but are underutilized. For example learning an instrument, doing brainteasers, puzzles or Sudoku if you never do it before, or done the problem before.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Learning a new language (might apply to programming languages as well?). Travelling to new environments/countries. Make art, music, creative endeavours (programming has a creative aspect; creativity within the bounds of logic). New exercises can build muscle memory and other benefits like balance coordination ,etc.

2. Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

Growth Mindset is the notion that we are not fixed by our inherent intelligence, abilities, skills we are born with. Growth mindset states that it is in our domain to change; another words we are the captain of our own ship. Roman philosopher Seneca said: "Luck is what happens when preparation meets opportunity." Which is a way of saying we make our own luck.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

No, It is something I have already thought about; I think there are some programmers who are extremely intelligent and come from highly scientific and mathematical backgrounds; however generally I find programming different from math; although it can involve complicated algorithms most are pretty simple. I think generally their is a mix of nature and nurture; people do start out with certain personality traits, or brain structures. (Allegedly 80% of the brain is formed by five years old).

Consistency is a super important aspect for both building muscle memory and getting better at solving problems; if you put it all down to a fixed mindset of not being able to change; then you never will. I have a believe that I can get better for example I took my typing speed from ~30wpm to ~60wpm by learning touch typing.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

By not getting discouraged if I do not learn something fast ,or figure something out straight off the bat ,or get stuck on a problem. I acknowledge that it takes time to get comfortable with being uncomfortable.
