I don't know maybe I know more than what I think I do in perspective; working on projects and trying to figure it out along the way is probably harder than doing a course to learn things. What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

Having values that roughly align with others in the workplace might create cohesion in a team; that is not to say all your values should or will be the same as others in a team, but enough common ground that you can co-exist. Being self aware is important otherwise you could end up rubbing people the wrong way and contribute to a toxic work environment. Being empathetic is important as a teacher because those that teach often were ~A+ students themselves so the content may have been easy to learn to them, or they just can't remember the struggle because they learnt programming at a young age; being able to put yourself in the perspective of a learner that knows nothing and is bombarded with new terminology that might be confusing.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Because you think it's important and that human skills can be taught?. But, I think that comes with time and is somewhat determined by personality and experiences in life.

Yeah, probably is a bit of waste of a time; compared to the opportunity cost, that's not to say that I do not think that social/human skills aren't important; However, I think they come over time and experiences in life, not five weeks focusing on the theory of human skills, although perhaps that makes you more aware of aspects of human skills and awareness is the first step in marketing.
