What is Emotional Intelligence?

Wikipedia says: "Emotional intelligence is most often defined as the ability to perceive, use, understand and manage emotions. People with high emotional intelligence can recognize their own emotions and those of others, use emotional information to guide thinking and behavior, discern between different feelings and label them appropriately, and adjust emotions to adapt to environments." So yeah basically being able to figure out how you are feeling (Which emotion you are feeling; if any. Unless your a sociopath/psycopath) and being able to read social situations and make others feel at ease and engaged with you.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is designed to measure individuals intelligence accross a range of standardized tests. I think people tend to associate IQ test with Ravens Progressive which measures abstract geometrical reasoning (to account for intelligence that may not be easily perceivable from verbal reasoning and language skills, e.g some people can't read, write, or speak) which in my opinion isn't really reflective of the problems that people face in the real world; of which I think the main one is effectively communicating with others which would argueably fall under Emotional intelligence. There are other tests falling under IQ that measure verbal reasoning, listening skills ,etc... Emotional intelligence is I think somewhat harder to measure because interactions with others are somewhat subjective and you have to read between the lines, it's easy to have a failure to communicate clearly and be understood; as to what you were trying to really say. There is a disconnect between thoughts and what comes out of someones mouth.

Why is it important?

Leading question that leaves no room for counter-argument.
